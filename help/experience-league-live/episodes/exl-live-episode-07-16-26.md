# AEM Content Fragments in Adobe Journey Optimizer: What's New and How to Use It

In this Experience League Live session, we walk through the latest enhancements to Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) Content Fragments in Adobe Journey Optimizer (AJO), so your content teams and journey practitioners can work together more efficiently, with less friction and more control. 

[![ExL LIVE July 16 2026](assets/WebBannerExlLive-July16-2026.png)](https://youtube.com/live/lrdorB0LgFo){target="_blank"}

Whether you're an AEM author managing a content library or an AJO practitioner building personalized journeys, this session covers what's new and how to put it to work. 

Join us to learn more on how to: 

Fetch Content Fragments from multiple AEM configurations, including author, publish, and authenticated publish tiers, and switch the source repository used across your journeys and campaigns. 

Manage fragment lifecycle from a new dedicated Content Fragments listing page in AJO, where you can identify out-of-sync fragments and trigger manual syncs to stay current. 

Preserve content fragment context throughout the message once a fragment is selected, so authors can reuse fragment fields across content blocks without reselecting. 

Work with locale and variation support to use alternate versions of the same content fragment more deliberately. 

Access AEM Content Fragments directly in AJO if you're on Managed Services. Add your AEM Managed Services repository URL once in configuration settings and you're ready. 
 

You'll leave with a clear picture of how AEM and AJO fit together in a production content workflow, and hands-on insight into the new capabilities that make fragments more usable, more governable, and more production-ready at scale. 

Ideal for AEM authors and AJO practitioners who collaborate on content and want to reduce handoff friction across teams.
